01 — THE SHORT VERSION

Most people either run the wires or write the code. I do both, because in Tanahun, if you don't, nobody does.

DG Link Network started as a fiber ISP for Shuklagandaki-4, Dulegauda — the kind of connection work that means climbing poles, tracing breaks in the line, and sitting with a customer until their router actually works. It's since grown to cover a second office in Bhimad.

The same instinct — "make this actually usable for someone in Nepal" — led to thenepal.io: small, fast tools like a Nepali font converter and a Bikram Sambat date converter, plus internal apps that run the operational side of the ISP itself.
